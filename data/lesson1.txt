Title: New Concept English Lesson 1 - A Private Conversation

Content: Last week, I went to the theatre. I had a very good seat. The play was very interesting. I did not enjoy it. A young man and a young woman were sitting behind me. They were talking loudly. I got very angry. I could not hear the actors. I turned round. I looked at the man and the woman angrily. They did not pay any attention. In the end, I could not bear it. I turned round again. "I can't hear a word!" I said angrily. "It's none of your business," the young man said rudely. "This is a private conversation!"

Grammar Points:
1. Simple Past Tense: "went", "had", "was", "did not enjoy" - Used to describe completed actions in the past.
2. Past Continuous Tense: "were sitting", "were talking" - Used to describe ongoing actions in the past.
3. Negative Sentences: "I did not enjoy it" - Using "did not + base verb" for past tense negatives.
4. Exclamatory Sentences: "I can't hear a word!" - Expressing strong emotion.

Vocabulary Notes:
- theatre: A building where plays and movies are shown. Example: "I went to the theatre to watch a play."
- seat: A place to sit. Example: "I had a very good seat in the front row."
- angry: Feeling or showing strong annoyance. Example: "I got very angry when they kept talking."
- attention: Notice; interest. Example: "They did not pay any attention to my complaint."
- private: Belonging to one particular person; not for public. Example: "This is a private conversation."
- business: In this context, "none of your business" means it does not concern you.
- bear: To tolerate or endure. Example: "I could not bear the noise anymore."
- rudely: In an impolite manner. Example: "The young man spoke rudely to me."

Key Expressions:
- "turn round" = to turn around
- "none of your business" = it doesn't concern you
- "I can't hear a word" = I cannot hear anything
- "pay attention" = to listen or watch carefully

Discussion Questions:
1. Why did the narrator get angry?
2. What does "a private conversation" mean in this context?
3. How would you feel if you were in the narrator's position?
